Egg Chaffle Sandwich

Egg Chaffle sandwiches have a great bready, crunchy texture from the chaffle and a nice creamy texture from the egg. They're the perfect breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

  • Serves: 1
  • Prep time: 5 mins
  • Cook time: 10 mins
  • Published: Feb 28, 2020

Ingredients

Chaffles:
  1. 1/2 cup grated cheese
  2. 1 egg
Eggs:
  1. 2 eggs
  2. 1 tbsp butter
  3. Salt and pepper

Instructions

Chaffles:
  1. Heat waffle iron.
    (I used a double waffle maker here. A belgian waffle maker or Dash waffle maker also work well. If your waffle maker has variable temperatures, these will cook best on a medium/low setting.)
  2. Combine grated cheese and egg in a bowl.
    (Virtaully any grated cheese (pre-grated or grated fresh from a block) can be used here. Just don't use anything that is very wet like fresh mozzarella.)
  3. Pour mixture into pre-heated waffle iron and close. The length of time these will take to cook depends upon your waffle iron. I just keep an eye on these and remove them from heat when they start to smell like a toasty cheesy bagel.
Eggs:
  1. Melt butter in pan over medium heat.
  2. Crack eggs into pan
  3. Season with salt and pepper
  4. Cook to desired doneness. Flip once if over easy is preferred.
Putting it all together:
  1. Place one prepared chaffle on a plate, top with cooked eggs, top with remaining chaffle, and enjoy!

Substitutions/Additions

  • Although these chaffles have a nice cheesy taste, a slice of cheese is a nice addition to any egg sandwich.
  • Bacon, sausage patties, or lox are a nice twist on this simple sandwich.
  • Avocado, tomato slices, baby spinach, arugula, or sprouts (depending on dietary tolerance) are also nice additions to this sandwich.
