Culichi Sauce

Culichi sauce is a great Mexican style creamy poblano sauce. It's very versatile, pairing well with beef, chicken, or seafood.

  • Serves: Many
  • Prep time: 1 hour
  • Cook time: 20 mins

Ingredients

  1. 2 poblano peppers
  2. 1/4 cup Cotija cheese
  3. 1 cup heavy cream
  4. Broth (for thinning)
  5. Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Blister poblanos over an open flame, outdoors. Using a grill is best for this. If you do not have a grill, you can place the poblanos in a covered baking dish and bake at 400°F until softened (about 40 mins). They won't have the char-grilled flavor but the sauce will still have a great peppery taste and creamy texture.
  2. Once peppers are cooked, set aside in a sealed container and let cool. Once cooled enough to handle, remove the skins, stems, and seeds.
  3. Rough chop peppers and add to stock pot along with heavy cream. Blend with an immersion blender until smooth. If you do have an immersion blender, this can also be done in a traditional blender.
  4. Heat sauce to simmer, adding broth to desired thickness.
  5. Once heated through, turn off the heat and stir in Cotija cheese.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste and serve.

Substitutions/Additions

  • If you do not have Cotija cheese, grated Parmesan cheese can be substituted in this recipe.
  • If you like your sauce more spicy, a spicier pepper like jalapeño or serrano can be added.
