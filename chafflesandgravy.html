Chaffles and Gravy

Chaffles (cheese waffles) and sausage gravy are the perfect pair! The crunchy, cheesy, bready texture of the chaffles are the perfect texture to stand up to the creamy, savory sausage gravy. Chaffles are so versatile, they can be eaten alone or with any number of pairings.

  • Serves: 1
  • Prep time: 5 mins
  • Cook time: 20 mins

Ingredients

Chaffles:
  1. 1/2 cup grated cheese
  2. 1 egg
Sausage Gravy:
  1. 2 oz ground pork
  2. 1 oz cream cheese
  3. 1 oz heavy whipping cream
  4. 1 oz broth
  5. Salt and pepper

Instructions

Chaffles:
  1. Heat waffle iron.
    (I used a double waffle maker here. A belgian waffle maker or Dash waffle maker also work well. If your waffle maker has variable temperatures, these will cook best on a medium/low setting.)
  2. Combine grated cheese and egg in a bowl.
    (Virtaully any grated cheese (pre-grated or grated fresh from a block) can be used here. Just don't use anything that is very wet like fresh mozzarella.)
  3. Pour mixture into pre-heated waffle iron and close. The length of time these will take to cook depends upon your waffle iron. I just keep an eye on these and remove them from heat when they start to smell like a toasty cheesy bagel.
Sausage Gravy:
  1. Sauté ground pork over medium heat until cooked thoroughly. Season with salt, pepper, and other seasonings to taste.
  2. Add remaining ingredients (cream cheese, haeavy cream, and broth), stir to combine, and heat over medium heat until well combined and desired texture is acheived. If your sauce becomes too thick, add more broth to thin it out. If your sauce becomes too watery, add more heavy cream to thicken it up and cook longer.
Putting it all together:
  1. Place prepared chaffles on a plate, top with sausage gravy, and enjoy!

Substitutions/Additions

  • You can substitute biscuits for the chaffles by following the Pizza crust recipe, following the instructions listed at the bottom of the recipe.
  • Seasonings for the sausage gravy are almost unlimited. A traditional twist is to add sage. One of my fav twists is to add a few dashes of hot sauce.
